How is Moa'men Zakria?

His football career

He started his football career with Al- Ahly from 2004 until 2008, then joined the Military Production Club in the 2010/2009 season, before moving to the Egyptian club at the beginning of the winter transfer period 2010/2011 season, in exchange for one million pounds for Military Production as a penalty clause.

Then he moved to Enppi during the winter transfer period, the 2012/2011 season, for one and a half million pounds.

Then he moved to Zamalek during the summer transfer period on loan from Enppi in the 2012/2013 season for one and a half million pounds, and a penalty clause in the event of Zamalek’s exit from the 2014 African Champions League returns to his club Enppi and won with Zamalek the 2014 Egypt Cup championship .

Then he moved to Al- Ahly Club during the winter transfer period of the 2014/2015 season for five million pounds and for five seasons from Enppi after the loan to Zamalek club ended .

What is the disease of Moa'men Zakria?

The story of Moamen Zakaria’s suffering from illness began, while he was loaned to the Saudi club Ohod in January 2019, and it was announced that the player suffers from a chronic injury that led to the termination of the loan, and Moamen moved away for treatment until the end of that season, as Moamen Zakaria suffers from amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, a disease of the muscles. It is rare that only one person in two million suffers from it, because of it a believer has moved away from playing football in the past two seasons. Muscular dystrophy is one of the rapidly spreading diseases in the body, as with the exacerbation of the injury, it moves from muscle atrophy to nerves, and thus the body loses control of the muscles, which affects the human body and all its senses, even in speech. Muscular dystrophy is treated according to its degree and the patient’s ability to move. Contrary to what is known, it is a disease that does not prevent a person from exercising completely. On the contrary, the patient must do sports according to the strength of the disease. Treatment of muscular dystrophy begins with surgical intervention in difficult cases, as well as taking medications and drugs, and finally exercising and adhering to physical therapy sessions.
